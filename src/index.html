What is the housing price - income ratio in Beijing?


When talking about housing market, housing affordability really measures. The price to income ratio is the basic affordability measure for housing in a given area. It is generally the ratio of median house prices to median familial disposable incomes, expressed as a percentage or as years of income. According to Citylab, in Boston, for example, the ratio is 5.2, which means median home in Boston cost 2.6 times as much as the median annual income.
